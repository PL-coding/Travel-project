Why Sydney?

Sydney, the vibrant city

A bright, modern city embedded in nature, where gallery-hopping, surfing, and fine-dining can all take place in a single day. A city of iconic attractions and natural beauty, Sydney is a destination you'll never forget.

To understand why this city is so singular, take the clifftop coastal walk from Bondi to Coogee, where you will pass by beaches, rock pools, and lush parks. Instead of limiting yourself to the well-known, make sure you enjoy the bush, parks, beaches and unique swimming spots.

Places to visit

Just a sample of the many amazing places to see

Bondi Beach to Bronte, or Coogee, walk

A cliff top coastal walk, the Bondi to Bronte walk extends for three kms, or six kms if you walk the whole route to Coogee. The walk features stunning views, beaches, parks, cliffs, bays and rock pools. The beaches and parks offer a place to rest, swim, surf or a chance to eat at one of the great cafes or restaurants.

Neilsen Park

Blessed with the glistening Shark Beach for swimming and glorious harbour views, it’s also one of the most picturesque places for a picnic. Relax on the grass and enjoy your lunch beneath a shady fig tree or take a 3.6km return walk along the unmissable Hermitage Foreshore track for ultimate views of Sydney Harbour.

Address:

6 Steele Point Rd, Vaucluse NSW 2030

Learn more

Royal Botanic Gardens

The Royal Botanic Garden is where Sydneysiders and visitors come to relax, be inspired and learn. You are encouraged to walk on the grass, touch the trees and smell the flowers. This oasis in the heart of the city is your opportunity to connect with the natural world and indulge your passion for plants or relax at one of the cafés or restaurants.

Address:

Mrs Macquaries Rd, Sydney NSW 2000
